FAQs

Question: How does the interview process work?
Answer: You will be given a set amount to time to choose five companies which you are most interested in via the Hacker Fellows portal. Afterwards, you will have the opportunity to phone interview with your top companies. Companies who wish to continue the interview process with you can set up an in-person interview on our scheduled interview day. From here it is up to the companies to proceed with the hiring process with you.

Question: Do I have to stay in the Wayne State dorms?
Answer: You are strongly encouraged you to take advantage of the paid room and board provided by Hacker Fellows at Wayne State. This is a great opportunity to spend time together with other Hacker Fellows and the cost of housing is part of your total compensation. You can choose to live elsewhere but you will be foregoing a chance to connect with other fellows!

Question: Do I have to take the Wayne State meal plan?
Answer: No. We recommend you take advantage of the 45 swipe meal plan because it will be very convenient and a great price, but it’s up to you! If you decline the provided meal plan, you will receive the cost of the meal plan as part of your stipend.

Question: How does the stipend work?
Answer: You’re receiving ~$60,000 in total annualized compensation as part of Hacker Fellows. During the training session, Hacker Fellows will directly pay for your WSU housing and parking, plus breakfast and dinner during the week. You’ll also receive a $1,000 stipend to cover lunch, weekend meals and incidentals. For those fellows that choose to not live in the provided housing, you will receive a $300 stipend for lunch.

Question: Will we have chances to network?
Answer: Yes! Hacker Fellows will host multiple happy hours and discussion panels with other developers and entrepreneurs. After the bootcamp is finished, fellows will be invited to multiple networking events around southeast Michigan as well as staying connected with other fellows.
